Chicken handi is a traditional Indian dish of boneless chicken in a creamy, tomato-based gravy with aromatic spicesThe name "handi" refers to the clay pot in which the dish is traditionally cooked, which gives it an earthy flavor. The gravy can be made with yogurt, dairy cream, coconut cream, cashew powder, or a combination of all.
Dal tadka is a popular Indian dish of cooked lentils finished with a tempering of spices and oil or ghee.The word dal means "lentils" in Hindi, and tadka means "tempering". The tempering technique involves frying spices and herbs in oil to release their flavor, aroma, and essential oils. The fried mixture is then added to the lentils, which changes the dish's taste, aroma, and flavor.
Motichur ladoo is a soft, melt-in-your-mouth, cuisine made from gram flour, sugar, ghee, nuts, saffron, and cardamom powder.The name motichur means "crushed pearls" in Hindi, and refers to the tiny fried balls of chickpea batter, or boondi, that are rolled into spheres and soaked in sugar syrup. Motichur ladoo is a popular sweet at Indian festivals and celebrations.
